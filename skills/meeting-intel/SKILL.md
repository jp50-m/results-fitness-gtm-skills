# Meeting Intel

Your AI chief of research. Walks into every meeting with the person already half-known.

## When to use this skill

Trigger when the user shares a name (or names) of someone they're about to meet, asks "who is X," or says they have a meeting coming up. Use it for prep AND post-meeting follow-up tracking.

## What to ask the user (first time only)

Save these as memory:

1. **What do you care about in a briefing?** (e.g., "their professional bio + 3 recent posts + something personal I can mention warmly")
2. **Default briefing length?** (one-page, half-page, 5 bullets)
3. **What you do, in one sentence** — so the briefing can suggest connection points
4. **Topics you don't want to bring up** (politics, religion, personal life beyond surface, etc.)

## Workflow — Pre-Meeting Briefing

When the user says "I have a meeting with [name]" or "research [name]":

1. **Gather public info** via web search:
   - LinkedIn profile (role, history, education)
   - Twitter/X recent activity (last 30 days, top 3 posts)
   - Podcast appearances (search "[name] podcast")
   - Press mentions (search "[name]" in news for last 90 days)
   - Personal site, Substack, or any owned media

2. **Cross-reference**: do you and they share any connections, schools, cities, or causes?

3. **Output a one-page briefing** in this format:

```
MEETING BRIEFING — [name]
For: [meeting type/context, if known]
Date: [meeting date]

WHO THEY ARE
  [2-3 sentence summary: role, company, what they're known for]

THE ARC
  [How they got here in 4-6 bullets — career trajectory]

WHAT THEY'RE TALKING ABOUT NOW (last 30-90 days)
  - [Recent post / podcast / launch / opinion]
  - [Recent post / podcast / launch / opinion]
  - [Recent post / podcast / launch / opinion]

OPENING THEY'D LIKELY APPRECIATE
  "[A specific, warm reference to something they recently did or said]"

POSSIBLE CONNECTION POINTS
  - You both [shared interest, school, city, mutual connection]
  - Their work on [X] overlaps with what you're building in [Y]

QUESTIONS THAT WILL LAND
  1. [Open-ended question that respects their expertise]
  2. [Question that creates an opening for them to talk about a current bet/project]
  3. [Question that surfaces what they'd want from this meeting]

WATCH OUT FOR
  - [Hot takes / public positions to be aware of]
  - [Anything you should NOT bring up — failed venture, controversy, etc.]

SOURCES
  [URLs to LinkedIn, podcast, articles]
```

4. **Save the briefing as a follow-up tracker entry** with the meeting date and the names.

## Workflow — Post-Meeting Follow-Up

When the user says "I just met with [name]" or "what's the follow-up":

1. **Pull the saved briefing** for that person.
2. **Ask the user**: "What did you commit to during the meeting?"
3. **Draft each follow-up** (intro emails, sending a resource, scheduling a next step) in their voice.
4. **Set a 7-day check-in**: "Want me to remind you in a week to nudge if they haven't responded?"
5. **Update the tracker** — what was discussed, what's outstanding.

## Rules

- Only use PUBLIC info. Never speculate about someone's personal life, finances, or anything they haven't shared publicly.
- Always cite sources at the bottom of the briefing — the user should be able to verify in 30 seconds.
- If the person has very little public footprint, say so directly. Don't pad. A one-line briefing is better than fluff.
- Flag any potentially sensitive context (recent controversy, public conflict, layoff at their company) so the user isn't blindsided.
- If the meeting is with a competitor, mark it clearly at the top.

## What "moving the needle" looks like

User walks into every meeting having read more about the person than the person expects, opens with a warm specific reference, and never lets a follow-up drop. After 30 days of use: more conversions, faster relationships, fewer awkward "remind me what you do?" moments.
